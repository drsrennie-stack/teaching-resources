Question Sections

Counts plus DOK levels

True / False

Bonus word format: One key term per statement is bolded and underlined. For false statements, correcting only that term earns 0.5 pt bonus. The answer key lists the correction for every false item.

Multiple Choice

Confidence-based: Students distribute 4 points across the options. Full directions printed on exam.
Standard: Students circle the best answer. No blanks in front of options.

Matching

Custom topics (optional)

Fill in the Blank

Fill in the blank format: Each sentence has one key term replaced with a blank line. The missing word is always a high-yield scientific term, value, or structure, never a common word. Answer key lists the correct term for every blank.

Free Response / Brain Dump

Choose ONE: All prompts are printed but students answer only ONE. Directions and points reflect the single-prompt total. Useful for student choice on essay sections.

Brain Dump options

Each question is a standalone free response prompt. Chart questions get a bordered table (columns chosen by topic). Non-chart questions get 5 or 6 blank writing lines.
Answer distribution: The AI writes the correct answer for each MC item. This tool then shuffles the option order client-side, rebuilds the letter for each answer, reports the actual A/B/C/D spread, guarantees no letter repeats more than twice in a row, and prints a key rebuilt from that shuffle. DOK levels are labeled and carried through to the key.

The AI is instructed to produce completely fresh questions for each version number. Same difficulty, different content, no repeated stems or scenarios.

Recommended ranges by exam type

Recall below 30
Orientation quizzes, pre-tests, first-week check-ins. Pure terminology and anatomy identification.
Foundational 30 to 50
Weekly module quizzes, early-semester unit checks. Mix of recall and basic application.
Moderate 51 to 70
Standard unit exams and midterms. Recommended default for most A&P I and II summative exams.
Challenging 71 to 85
Cumulative finals, comprehensive midterms. Heavy DOK 2 to 3 synthesis across systems. Use when you want meaningful separation between prepared and unprepared students.
Advanced above 85
Pre-professional prep, USMLE/NCLEX practice, doctoral-level integrative exams. Heavy DOK 3 to 4. Not recommended for standard A&P without scaffolding.
